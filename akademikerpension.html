AkademikerPension

AkademikerPension is a pension fund for Danish and non-Danish citizens, employed mainly in the public sector e. g. universities and upper secondary schools. It covers academics, that is, people with Danish M.As, M.Scs and PhDs. The pension fund also admits members from the private sector with academic degrees in the above-mentioned areas and currently has more than 140.000 members.

Role

Front-end designer and developer

Client

AkademikerPension

Year

2020

THE ISSUE

PROBLEM FORMULATION

How might we make a digital idea that will enhance all our members pension knowledge and inspire them to continually change their own products and insurances when their life changes?

THE PROCESS

FROM SCRATCH TO INITIAL DESIGN

1.
Research

The team began by doing some research on direct competitors to pinpoint the issue or find out hints of what could be done better and how. Based upon that research, we created a survey and had the target audience answer a few questions.

2.
Translation

Based upon the research done, personas and user journeys were created to understand the users better and furthermore analyse the core issue.

3.
Ideation

Once the issue was found, using the Theory of Change model was a natural next step to describe and illustrature how and why a desired change is expected to happen in a particular content, which lead us to do some Card Sorting to reorganise the navigation by the usersâ€™ standards.

4.
Wireframes

Last but not least, wireframes were made based on research and ideation to showcase what the ideal shape of our ideas would be.

THE SOLUTION

PROTOTYPE

The designed solution was created with a few things in mind: the hero banner was to be removed, the cards needed to be smaller, concise and more captivating and the subpages of each category needed to be redone and reorganised completely.

To replace the lengths of body copy, a short video was placed at the top as a short explanation, with a back-up plan right after as the steps are explained in a short and direct manner.

Finally, the user is given advice that stands out and can find an accordion with the most asked questions under the category or simply reach out to customer support, all without needing to go very far.
